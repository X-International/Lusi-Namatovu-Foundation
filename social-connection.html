Breaking the Silence of Isolation

In Rakai District, many elderly individuals face the heavy burden of social isolation and loneliness, often due to limited mobility, the loss of loved ones, or lack of transportation. These challenges can lead to feelings of disconnection, taking a toll on their physical and mental well-being.

The Lusi Namatovu Foundation's Social Connection program is dedicated to combating this isolation by fostering social interaction, companionship, and a sense of belonging. Through meaningful engagement, we aim to ensure every elder feels valued, supported, and part of their community.

Our Goals: Building Connections

We aim to create meaningful connections for Rakai's elderly through targeted, community-driven goals:

Social Events

Organize 4 social gatherings and events each month, fostering joy and interaction through community meals, music sessions, and cultural celebrations.

Home Visits

Provide 10 home visits and companionship sessions per week, offering support to those with limited mobility and ensuring no one feels alone.

Support Groups

Establish 3 elderly support groups in the community, creating safe spaces for shared experiences and lasting friendships.

Intergenerational Activities

Facilitate 2 intergenerational activities per year, bridging generations through storytelling and shared community projects.

Community Awareness

Raise awareness about social connection through community outreach, emphasizing the importance of elderly well-being and inclusion.

How We Foster Connection

Our comprehensive approach to creating meaningful social connections:

Social Gatherings and Events

We host regular social gatherings where elderly individuals can connect, share laughter, and enjoy a sense of community. These events include:

  • Community meals and cooking sessions
  • Traditional music and dance celebrations
  • Cultural festivals and holiday gatherings
  • Group prayer and spiritual activities

Home Visits and Companionship

Our volunteers and staff provide weekly home visits to elderly individuals who are isolated or have limited mobility. Services include:

  • Friendly conversation and emotional support
  • Assistance with light household tasks
  • Reading and sharing stories
  • Health and well-being check-ins

Elderly Support Groups

We facilitate support groups where individuals can share experiences and build lasting friendships. Groups focus on:

  • Shared experiences and mutual support
  • Skills sharing and learning
  • Health and wellness discussions
  • Community project planning

Intergenerational Activities

We bring elderly individuals and younger generations together through activities that foster understanding:

  • Storytelling and wisdom sharing sessions
  • Joint community improvement projects
  • Cultural skills and crafts workshops
  • Mentoring and guidance programs

Who Can Join Our Program

Our Social Connection program is open to elderly individuals in Rakai who meet the following criteria:

Age Requirement

Be over the age of 60 years.

Location

Reside in Rakai District, Uganda.

Social Interaction Need

Express a desire for increased social interaction and companionship.

Support Need

Face challenges like limited mobility or lack of family support that contribute to isolation.
