Hunger Challenges in Rakai District

Many elderly individuals and people with disabilities in Rakai District face food insecurity due to limited income, mobility challenges, and lack of support. This often leads to malnutrition and declining health.

Our Food Security Program provides nutritious meals and sustainable solutions to ensure they have access to the food they need, improving their health and dignity.

Learn More About Our Program

Our Goals for Food Security

  • Provide food assistance to 400 elderly individuals and people with disabilities monthly.

  • Distribute 500 food packages with essential staples each month.

  • Organize 10 community meals per month for social and nutritional support.

  • Support 100 elderly individuals and people with disabilities to establish home gardens.

  • Educate 800 community members on nutrition and healthy eating practices.

Support Our Goals

How We Fight Hunger

Food Package Distribution

We distribute food packages with essentials like maize, beans, rice, and cooking oil to elderly individuals and people with disabilities. These packages ensure a balanced diet to combat malnutrition.

Community Meals

We host monthly community meals where elderly individuals can enjoy hot, nutritious food and companionship. These gatherings foster social connection and well-being.

Home Garden Support

We help elderly individuals and people with disabilities establish home gardens by providing seeds, tools, and training. This promotes sustainable food production and independence.

Nutrition Education

We offer nutrition workshops to teach the community about healthy eating on a budget. Topics include balanced diets, meal preparation, and preventing malnutrition.

Who Qualifies for Our Program

  • Be 60 years or older, or an adult with a disability.

  • Reside in Rakai District, Uganda.

  • Face challenges accessing food due to poverty, limited mobility, or lack of support.

  • Experience food insecurity or malnutrition affecting health and well-being.
