The Spark That Started It All

A Legacy of Compassion

A Heartfelt Discovery

The Lusi Namatovu Foundation was founded in 2016 by Samuel Ssebandeke, a compassionate individual deeply moved by the challenges faced by elderly people in Rakai District. During a visit from the UK, Samuel found his elderly aunt, Christina Namazzi, in a dire situation—neglected by her family, with no access to basic care or support. Her struggle, coupled with the harsh reality that many elders in Uganda lack government assistance after retirement, ignited a fire in Samuel to take action and bring change to his community.

Turning Pain into Purpose

"I realized that if my own aunt could be in such a situation, there must be many more elderly people suffering in silence. We had to do something."

— Samuel Ssebandeke, Founder

Driven by a vision of a community where elderly individuals are valued and cared for, Samuel took the first steps to establish an organization that would provide essential support and advocate for their well-being. In 2017, he registered the foundation as a Community Based Organization in memory of his late mother, Lusi Namatovu, vowing to work hand-in-hand with the community to improve the lives of senior citizens.

The Early Days

Initially, the foundation focused on elderly care, providing shelter, food, clothing, and medical care to a small group of vulnerable elders in Rakai District. A dedicated team, led by Samuel, went door-to-door, delivering essentials like rice and sugar to those who had once begged for survival. These early efforts laid the groundwork for a growing movement to bring dignity and hope to the region's neglected seniors, setting the stage for the foundation's expanded mission to also support people with disabilities.

See How We've Grown

A Milestone of Commitment

In 2016, the Lusi Namatovu Foundation achieved a pivotal milestone by officially registering as a Community-Based Organization (CBO) in Uganda. This registration solidified our commitment to serving the Rakai community, creating a foundation for sustainable operations and growth. Named in memory of Samuel Ssebandeke's late mother, Lusi Namatovu, a woman who embodied compassion and dedication, the foundation's name serves as a constant reminder of our core values—dignity, care, and respect for those we support.

Our Journey

Key Milestones in Our Growth

2016

The Foundation Begins

Samuel Ssebandeke founds the Lusi Namatovu Foundation, starting with small-scale distributions of food and clothing to elders in Rakai.

2017

Official Registration

The foundation is registered as a Community Based Organization, expanding its outreach to more elderly individuals in Rakai District.

2018

Growing Impact

The foundation reaches over 100 elderly beneficiaries, providing monthly meals, medical care, and shelter support.

2024

Expanding Our Mission

The foundation broadens its mission to include support for people with disabilities, starting with the distribution of wheelchairs and crutches.

2025

Looking Ahead

Aiming to support 50+ individuals with disabilities and expand elderly care to nearby districts in Uganda.
