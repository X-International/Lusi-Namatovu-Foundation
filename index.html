Who We Are

Serving Rakai's Most Vulnerable
With Compassion

Established in 2016 and deeply rooted in Rakai District, Uganda, the Lusi Namatovu Foundation champions the well-being of vulnerable elderly individuals and people with disabilities. We believe everyone deserves to be seen, supported, and live with dignity and respect, regardless of age or ability, especially those often overlooked within our rural communities.

Our dedicated team provides essential, compassionate support tailored to diverse needs. This includes ensuring food security, safe housing assistance, and healthcare access for elders, alongside vital companionship to combat isolation. For people with disabilities, we facilitate access to assistive devices, therapy services, and advocate for inclusion, empowering independence and full participation in community life.

The Need In Rakai

Key Challenges Driving Our Mission

Rakai District presents significant challenges for its most vulnerable, underscoring an urgent need for dedicated community support and intervention.

Elderly Isolation & Neglect

Without adequate pensions or family networks, many elders face deep isolation, poor nutrition, and unreliable healthcare access during their vulnerable later years in Rakai District.

Learn More

Disability Barriers & Exclusion

Significant barriers often prevent people with disabilities from accessing essential healthcare, vital resources, and key opportunities, frequently leading to social stigma and exclusion.

Learn More

Poverty & Resource Scarcity

Pervasive poverty exacerbates these struggles, leaving fundamental needs like reliable food, secure housing, and consistent medical treatment tragically out of reach for many individuals.

Learn More
