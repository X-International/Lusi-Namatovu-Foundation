Our Founding Story

The Journey of Compassion

The journey of the Lusi Namatovu Foundation began with a personal experience. In 2016, during a visit from the UK, Samuel Ssebandeke found his elderly Aunt Christina Namazzi in difficult circumstances due to neglect. This heartbreaking encounter opened his eyes to the often-hidden struggles faced by many elders in Rakai District lacking adequate support, sparking a desire to make a tangible difference.

Driven by this purpose, the foundation was formally established and registered as a Community Based Organisation (CBO) in 2017. It was named in loving memory of Samuel's late mother, Lusi Namatovu, reflecting a deep commitment to care and family values. Initially, our efforts centered entirely on providing essential support—shelter, food, medication, and companionship—to vulnerable senior citizens often forgotten by society.

As our work deepened within the community, we recognized that people with disabilities often face similar, profound challenges of neglect, poverty, and isolation. In recent years, guided by a desire for greater inclusion and impact, we expanded our mission. Today, the Lusi Namatovu Foundation proudly serves both elderly individuals and people with disabilities, striving to create a more supportive and equitable Rakai for all vulnerable residents, as our journey of compassion continues to evolve while we respond to community needs.

Our Guiding Principles

The Core of Our Commitment

Our Mission, Vision, and Core Values are the bedrock of our work, guiding every action we take to support and uplift the Rakai community.

Our Mission

We are dedicated to empowering Rakai's elderly and people with disabilities through compassionate, community-focused action. Our mission provides essential resources, promotes fundamental dignity, fosters greater independence, and ensures full community participation for those most vulnerable, actively working to break cycles of neglect.

Our Vision

We envision a Rakai where every elder and person with a disability thrives, their inherent worth deeply valued and their lives fulfilling. Our future sees them fully included, supported by sustainable community systems that actively champion their rights, ensuring well-being for all generations.

Our Core Values

The Principles We Stand By

These core principles guide every aspect of our work, shaping our actions and relationships as we serve the Rakai community with dedication and respect.

Dignity

Upholding the inherent worth and promoting self-respect for every individual we serve.

Compassion

Acting with empathy, kindness, and understanding towards our beneficiaries and community.

Inclusion

Ensuring equitable access and full participation for people of all ages and abilities.

Community

Fostering a sense of belonging, mutual support, and collective well-being within Rakai.

Integrity

Operating with transparency, honesty, and accountability in all our actions and relationships.

Empowerment

Enabling individuals we support to gain independence, voice, and control over their own lives.
