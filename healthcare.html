Healthcare Challenges in Rakai District

Many elderly individuals and people with disabilities in Rakai District struggle to access healthcare. Financial barriers, limited mobility, and a lack of nearby services make it difficult to manage chronic conditions and maintain their health.

Our Healthcare & Medication Program provides accessible, quality care tailored to their needs. Through regular medical checkups, medication support, and community outreach, we help improve their health and quality of life.

Learn More About Our Program

Our Goals for Better Healthcare

  • Provide regular medical checkups to 500 elderly individuals and people with disabilities annually.

  • Distribute essential medications to 300 individuals with chronic conditions each year.

  • Conduct 20 health education workshops on preventive care for the community.

  • Support 150 elderly individuals and people with disabilities with transportation to medical appointments.

  • Advocate for improved healthcare policies and services in Rakai District.

Support Our Goals

How We Make a Difference

Medical Checkups and Treatment

We provide regular medical checkups for elderly individuals and people with disabilities, including blood pressure monitoring, diabetes screening, and eye exams. Our team also offers treatment for common illnesses and injuries, with referrals to specialists when needed.

Medication Support

We distribute essential medications to elderly individuals and people with disabilities with chronic conditions like hypertension, diabetes, and arthritis. Our staff ensures proper usage through education and ongoing monitoring.

Health Education Workshops

We host health education workshops to promote preventive care and healthy living for the community. Topics include nutrition, exercise, hygiene, and disease prevention.

Transportation Assistance

We offer transportation support to elderly individuals and people with disabilities who struggle to reach medical appointments due to mobility challenges or lack of access.

Advocacy for Better Healthcare

We advocate for improved healthcare policies and services at local and regional levels, ensuring better access and support for elderly individuals and people with disabilities in Rakai District.

Who Qualifies for Our Program

  • Be 60 years or older, or an adult with a disability.

  • Reside in Rakai District, Uganda.

  • Face challenges accessing healthcare due to financial constraints, limited mobility, or lack of nearby services.

  • Have a chronic health condition requiring ongoing medical support, such as hypertension, diabetes, or mobility impairments.
